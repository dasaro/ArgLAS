## Lens verdicts

**Theory and proofs.** The theory core is sound and, for a learning-systems paper, unusually careful: I re-checked the Appendix A completion computations for all three semantics in both directions (they are correct), Proposition 2's (a)=(b)=(c) argument, the BAF abstraction-plus-splitting route, and Theorem 3's reduction — no errors found, and Theorem 3's 'equivalently' is properly discharged by Proposition 2. The paper's exposure is not correctness but COVERAGE and ANCHORING: (1) the theorems certify canonical programs while the flagship campaign learns constraint-style presentations whose correctness is asserted in prose and sample-checked only — the paper's own 'proofs, not spot checks' moral cuts against it here; (2) the 'unified background' that headlines Q1/C1 is never exhibited, its VAF fragment is internally inconsistent as described and absent from the repo, and VAF gets neither experiment nor theorem despite the theorem being an hours-cheap corollary of the Theorem 2 pattern; (3) the reference side of the equivalence chain is anchored by citation for AAF and by nothing for BAF; (4) the ABA proposition's proof rests on an unexhibited enumeration program and an undefined AS*-minimization variant; (5) Fages-with-constraints and partial evaluation of input atoms are used as folklore without lemma or pinpoint citation. Every one of these is repairable in hours to a few days, almost entirely in-paper (one optional VAF smoke experiment reuses the existing breadth pipeline). Fixing findings 1-6 would make Section 4 airtight against a logician referee; the MINOR items are statement hygiene that a thorough review would enumerate. Nothing here is a rejection-level defect, but findings 1 and 2 together (guarantees not attaching to learned artifacts; headline unified-B/VAF claim unexhibited and partly inconsistent) would likely anchor a major-revision verdict if left unaddressed.

**Experiment 1 design.** Experiment 1 is, by ILP-evaluation standards, unusually well-engineered: grouped framework-disjoint CV, a first-class failure taxonomy with an honest worst-case censoring bound on the main grid, pinned seeds, committed data, and a breadth section that reports its own boundary (PRF on self-attacks) rather than hiding it. No internal-validity defect found here invalidates the headline findings (E1.1-E1.3). However, a tough AIJ reviewer will land on a consistent theme: the evidential surface is narrower than the prose. Concretely: (1) the submission is not yet self-contained — Appendix C is a stub with a visible todo and the B2 negative-calibration table the bridge leans on appears nowhere (BLOCKER, but cheap to fix from committed data); (2) the near-1.0 recovery numbers are scored against uniform-random test negatives that are mostly trivially rejectable, so the metric's discriminative power against approximately-correct theories is unquantified — a hard-negative (Hamming-1) test slice is the single most valuable addition; (3) everything rests on one generator family, one master-seed realization, one learner binary with default unstated flags, one noise mechanism (which moreover differs from the per-argument noise model the bridge's own calibration used), and the family-breadth claims (BAF/ABA) are established only at clean, complete labels with tiny test yields. Each of these has a low-cost remedy that reuses existing machinery (the v3 anchor-slice config pattern, the flip_one policy, the config-driven ILASP flags, evaluation-only cross-pool transfer scoring), and several are pure analysis over the already-committed CSVs (fold-bootstrap CIs, the MCC vs MCC_FULL wedge, the large-regime worst-case bound). Estimated total remediation: roughly two to three weeks of effort, of which perhaps one week is compute that can run unattended; prioritize the BLOCKER, the hard-negative slice, the cross-generator transfer, and replicate seeds for the headline cells — together they convert the paper's main vulnerability (breadth of prose vs narrowness of evidence) into its strongest asset.

**Statistics and reporting.** Statistically this is an unusually candid empirical paper: a first-class failure taxonomy, a worst-case censoring sensitivity analysis, disclosed pipeline bugs, an honestly flagged underpowered sign test, and t-corrected fold CIs on the slice figures. The remaining exposure is concentrated in five places. Two are submission-stopping in the literal sense: the B2 negative-calibration claim and Appendix B are still \todo stubs, so load-bearing methodology is currently unverifiable. The most substantive statistical issue is the headline 'noise is bought back by data' claim: I verified against data/exp1_v2 that the q=0.2, f=80 pooled cell rests on 2/20 completions in the p=0.5 stratum, so Table 3's pooled row is compositionally a p>=0.75 mean — but I also verified the claim holds cleanly and uncensored at p>=0.75, so a stratified presentation fixes it entirely with committed data. In Experiment 2 the significance narrative switches reading and unit where convenient — the primary (committed-only, deduplicated) test is null at p=0.71 — and needs a single robustness table with declared test families, effect sizes, and a participant-clustered variant; all recomputable without learning runs. Finally, the PAR-2 comparison has no numbers at all, and uncertainty reporting is uneven (central table and curves bare, slice figures equipped). Every fix above except the B2 calibration re-run and an optional ICCMA re-run is a re-analysis or presentation change over the committed data/ record, i.e., hours each; nothing found threatens the direction of any conclusion.

**Experiment 2 / human semantics.** The Exp2/human-semantics arc (Sections 6–8) is unusually self-critical for a submission — the gate methodology, the disclosed audit of the authors' own inflated first pass, the estimand caveats, and the honest Limitations paragraph will all earn reviewer goodwill. But the arc has two structural soft spots a tough AIJ reviewer will drive at. First, the comparison to CF2 is asymmetrically instrumented: grounded and stable are gated, CF2 — the pre-registered comparator and the object of the headline conclusion — is not, and the paper's own evidence (SCC predicates 'ignored') hints CF2 may not be expressible in the hypothesis space, which would confound both 'learning does not beat CF2' and the 'not cycle bookkeeping' inference; a CF2 gate is mandatory. Second, the inferential layer is thinner than the narrative: a literal TODO where the negative-strategy calibration table should be, no confidence intervals on any Exp2 number including the '80% CF2 ceiling', no power/MDE statement behind six of seven per-condition nulls, an in-corpus feature selection defended only by a promised future replication when a leave-condition-out validation is runnable today, and a headline novel finding (the smoke/fire hedge) resting on 3/5 folds with no direct behavioural test. The good news is that nearly every remediation is cheap: the corpus, per-response predictions, and even the needed scripts (synthetic_neg_robustness.py, pc3_holm_power.py, per_condition_experiment.py, estimate_operating_point.py, consensus_extract.py) already exist, so the CI/power/ceiling/LOCO/stability work is hours-to-days of re-scoring and FastLAS reruns. The only genuinely new investments are the CF2 gate (days), a negative-policy ablation on the human data (days), and an out-of-corpus zero-shot test of the frozen σ_H on a second public dataset (Cramer–Guillaume cognitive-study data, Rahwan 2010 reinstatement chains; 1–2 weeks) — the last being the single highest-leverage addition, since it converts 'The Semantics People Follow' from a seven-graph description into a validated behavioural theory and simultaneously discharges the promised pre-registered replication.

**Baselines and comparative evaluation.** Section 8 is the weakest section of an otherwise well-fortified paper, and it is weak in a structural way: both headline comparisons (ICCMA timing and AGNN data efficiency) are restated from the authors' own unrefereed preliminary report rather than re-run, the timing paragraph contains literally no numbers, and the AGNN 30-example collapse -- the load-bearing quantitative fact behind the paper's 'interpretable learning is the only viable option when data is scarce' thesis -- has no reproducible artifact in the repo. The one comparison that IS re-run for this paper (the feature-classifier baseline, analysis/tree_baseline/) is genuinely well-designed on protocol (same 500-framework pool, grouped 5-fold CV, majority floor, committed artifacts) but has three exploitable soft spots: an all-local feature set that makes the grounded-target ceiling an artifact of withheld features rather than task hardness (grounded acceptance is poly-time computable); a noise claim that splices the most fragile model's numbers while the artifact shows logistic regression is noise-robust on the same target; and a LAS comparison value (MCC 1.0) that is asserted by construction rather than measured on the same per-argument rows. The 'cannot represent the set of extensions' impossibility claim is refutable by citation (neural extension enumeration exists), and the Popper/Aleph dismissal contains a factually wrong noise clause that an ILP reviewer will catch. None of this threatens the paper's core contributions (the proofs and Experiments 1-2 stand on their own), but Section 8 as written would draw a major-revision demand from any reviewer who opens the artifact or knows the ILP/neuro-argumentation literature. The good news: every fix is cheap relative to the campaign work already done -- the two expensive items (AGNN rerun on the paper's own pool, ICCMA timing table) total roughly two weeks; the remaining fixes (k-hop features + rerun, LAS row computed on identical folds, per-model noise table, brave-induction rewrite of the Popper dismissal) are hours-to-days each and mostly strengthen the paper's own thesis (e.g., the k-hop ablation converts the grounded result into a sharper bounded-depth-vs-fixpoint argument).

**Positioning and framing.** Through the positioning/novelty/framing lens this is a strong, unusually honest paper whose main exposure is not error but claim-calibration: three of its four headline framings (the 'no prior work' novelty claim, the 'no semantics encodes this hedge' sigma_H claim, and the proved-correct/robustly-recovered juxtaposition) are stated one notch broader than what is demonstrated, and each has a concrete, cheap repair. The single submission-blocking item is mechanical: an unresolved TODO leaves the B2 negative-calibration license — the methodological foundation of Experiment 2's design — with no reported evidence. The most valuable improvements per unit effort are (i) hedging the novelty claim and citing the ABML/neural-symbolic/graded-and-ranking-based lines it currently ignores (hours, removes the two most attackable sentences in the paper), (ii) the sigma_H principle-profile audit and the campaign-hypothesis exact-equivalence audit (each 2-3 days on existing machinery in analysis/zlatina_theorems, and each converts a rhetorical hedge into a result), and (iii) closing the GRD scope-out with the already-working sparse/definite-background experiment from analysis/grd_prf_lab. The VAF gap in Q1 must be resolved in one direction or the other (small experiment or demotion of the claim). Section 8's imported, non-re-run comparisons are the one place the paper's otherwise exemplary evidential hygiene visibly lapses and a referee will notice the contrast. None of the findings threatens the paper's core results; all threaten how much a skeptical reviewer will trust its framing of them.
